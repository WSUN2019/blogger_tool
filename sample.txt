SUBTITLE: A quiet rotation for daily writing

# The Short Version

Fountain pens slow you down in the right way. Here's the rotation that stuck after three months of daily notes, journaling, and meeting scribbles.

## Quick Take

If you're starting out, skip the deep end — a Pilot Metropolitan and a bottle of Iroshizuku will teach you more than any premium pen.

> Tip: Nib size matters more than price. An EF nib on a cheap pen beats a broad nib on an expensive one if you write small.

# The Daily Drivers

These are the pens I reach for without thinking.

- **Pilot Custom 74** — Japanese soft-medium nib, resin body, understated. The one I'd keep if I could only have one.
- **TWSBI Eco** — Demonstrator with a huge ink capacity. The workhorse; I fill it with Iroshizuku Take-Sumi and don't think about it for weeks.
- **Lamy 2000** — Bauhaus legend. Hooded nib, Makrolon body, wet writer. Polarizing ergonomics but I love it.
- **Platinum 3776 Century** — Slip-and-seal cap means it writes instantly even after a month uncapped.
- **Sailor Pro Gear Slim** — The 14k nib has that faint feedback Sailor is famous for. My desk pen.

# Ink Notes

A few inks have earned permanent spots in the drawer.

- **Iroshizuku Take-Sumi** — The best black I've used. Neutral, no sheen, dries fast.
- **Diamine Oxford Blue** — A proper navy. Work-appropriate but not boring.
- **Sailor Jentle Yama-dori** — Teal with red sheen on good paper. Show-off ink.

> Note: Rotate inks through pens every month or so. Stagnant ink in a pen you don't use is the fastest way to a clogged feed.

# What I Learned

Three months in, a few things clicked.

## On Nib Width

Buy narrower than you think. Japanese Fine ≈ Western Extra Fine. I kept buying Mediums and wishing I'd bought Fines.

## On Paper

Paper matters more than the pen. Rhodia, Tomoe River, and Midori MD make any pen look good. Copy paper makes even a $500 pen feel mediocre.

## On Price

After about $150 you're paying for finish and nostalgia, not writing quality. The Pilot Custom 74 outwrites pens three times its price.

FOOTER: 420 dollars, 5 pens, and more ink than any reasonable person needs.
